# Stylometry Analysis Results Documentation

## Overview
This document summarizes the results from the stylometry analysis performed on poetry datasets from Project Gutenberg and Poetry Foundation. The analysis includes dataset exploration, basic statistics, and unsupervised clustering of poems based on stylistic features.

## Datasets Analyzed

### Project Gutenberg Catalog
- **Source**: https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/feeds/pg_catalog.csv
- **Total Records**: 77,962
- **Dominant Language**: English (79.3% of records)
- **Poetry Shelf Share**: 6.2% (4,860 records identified as poetry)

**Key Insights**:
- Heavily skewed toward English and public-domain works
- Poetry represents a small but significant portion of the catalog
- Could bias AI models toward canonical Anglo forms

### Poetry Foundation Dataset
- **Source**: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/TGDivy/WebScrapping-PoetryFoundation/master/PoetryFoundationData.csv
- **Total Poems**: 13,854
- **Median Length**: 156 words (mean: 255 words)
- **Median Lines**: 16 (mean: 26 lines)

**Key Insights**:
- Many poems are short enough to be convincingly generated by current LLMs
- This could amplify issues with AI-generated content and authorship attribution

## Stylometry Analysis

### Features Computed
For each poem, the following stylistic features were calculated:
- **Basic metrics**: word count, line count, average words per line/sentence
- **Lexical diversity**: Type-Token Ratio (TTR), hapax ratio (unique words used once)
- **Readability**: Flesch Reading Ease proxy (approximate)
- **Structural features**: punctuation density, uppercase ratio, stopword ratio
- **Poetic devices**: end-rhyme score (crude measure of line-ending repetition)

### Clustering Results
Poems were clustered into 6 groups using K-means on standardized features, with 2D visualization via PCA.

#### PCA Visualization
The clusters show clear separation in the 2D PCA space, indicating that the stylometric features effectively distinguish different poetic styles. The projection captures the main variance in the feature space, with PC1 and PC2 representing combinations of length, complexity, and structural features.

#### Cluster Characteristics

**Cluster 0 (n=157)**:
- Longest poems: median 1,746 words, 250 lines
- Lower lexical diversity (TTR: 0.406)
- Moderate end-rhyme (0.640)
- Lower readability (Flesch: 69.3)

**Cluster 1 (n=48)**:
- Very long single-line poems: median 3,701 words, 1 line
- Lowest lexical diversity (TTR: 0.339)
- No end-rhyme (0.000)
- Lower readability (Flesch: 61.2)

**Cluster 2 (n=6,433) - Largest cluster**:
- Short poems: median 235 words, 25 lines
- Moderate lexical diversity (TTR: 0.590)
- Moderate end-rhyme (0.287)
- Higher readability (Flesch: 75.2)

**Cluster 3 (n=513)**:
- Short poems: median 226 words, 33 lines
- High lexical diversity (TTR: 0.601)
- Moderate end-rhyme (0.248)
- Very low readability (Flesch: -115.6) - likely due to complex language

**Cluster 4 (n=6,602) - Second largest**:
- Very short poems: median 111 words, 8 lines
- Highest lexical diversity (TTR: 0.761)
- Low end-rhyme (0.094)
- Moderate readability (Flesch: 68.3)

**Cluster 5 (n=101)**:
- Empty/invalid poems: median 0 words, 0 lines
- All metrics at 0 or minimal values

### Cluster Interpretation
- **Cluster 0**: Long, narrative poems with some rhyme
- **Cluster 1**: Prose-like long poems (possibly free verse epics)
- **Cluster 2**: Standard short-to-medium length poems
- **Cluster 3**: Complex, vocabulary-rich short poems
- **Cluster 4**: Very concise, diverse vocabulary poems (haiku-like)
- **Cluster 5**: Data quality issues (empty poems)

### Top Poets by Cluster
- **Cluster 0**: William Carlos Williams, John Ashbery, Wallace Stevens
- **Cluster 2**: Sylvia Plath, E.E. Cummings, Robert Frost
- **Cluster 3**: John Ashbery, Wallace Stevens, William Carlos Williams
- **Cluster 4**: William Carlos Williams, E.E. Cummings, Sylvia Plath

## AI vs Human Classification Summary (Final Results)

### Dataset
- **Human poems:** 130 randomly sampled from Poetry Foundation
- **AI poems:** 130 hand-crafted by human author (15 thematic clusters × ~13 poems)
- **Total analyzed:** 260 poems
- **Features:** 12 stylometry metrics + 384-dim semantic embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) = 396 total
- **Preprocessing:** SimpleImputer (median), StandardScaler
- **Classifier:** LogisticRegression (L2, max_iter=1000)

### Stylometric Signatures of Hand-Crafted AI Poems

**High-Impact Features (Estimated):**
1. **punctuation_density** — Consistent dash/colon usage (metaphorical structure)
2. **avg_words_per_line** — Uniform line length patterns
3. **end_rhyme_ish** — Subtle line-ending repetition
4. **readability_flesch_proxy** — Slightly higher (clearer structure)
5. **ttr (Type-Token Ratio)** — Moderate, consistent diversity

**Semantic Signatures:**
- **Thematic clustering:** 15 distinct themes (moonlight, nature, love, memory, etc.)
- **Vocabulary consistency** within themes
- **Metaphor patterns** (water/memory, neon/solitude, transformation/time)
- **Emotional register** highly consistent within and across poems

### Risk Assessment & Limitations

**What This Result Means:**
- Hand-crafted AI poems have a **strong, easily detectable stylistic fingerprint**
- The combination of consistent punctuation, line structure, and semantic patterns makes them distinguishable
- Performance is near-perfect (0.993 AUC) but under optimal conditions

**Generalization Risks:**
1. **Single-author AI bias:** All poems crafted by one human; lacks diversity of real AI outputs
2. **Optimal test conditions:** Human poems are random samples; real adversarial AI might score differently
3. **Thematic structure:** AI poems intentionally organized by theme; reduces variability
4. **LLM comparison:** Real LLM-generated poetry (GPT-2, GPT-4) may have different signatures
5. **Model fingerprinting:** High performance may reflect this specific style rather than general AI poetry

**Not Tested:**
- Adversarial poems (written to fool classifier)
- Real LLM-generated poems
- Cross-corpus generalization (other human poetry sources)
- Different author's hand-crafted poetry

### Recommended Robustness Checks

1. **Adversarial testing:** Human author attempts to write poems that fool classifier
2. **LLM comparison:** Generate poems with GPT-2, GPT-3.5, GPT-4 and evaluate detection
3. **Cross-corpus validation:** Test on Gutenberg poems, modern poetry, classical period
4. **Feature ablation:** Train on stylometry only (12-dim) vs embeddings only (384-dim)
5. **Explainability:** Extract LogisticRegression coefficients to identify key distinguishing features
6. **Low-confidence analysis:** Inspect poems near decision boundary (0.45–0.55 detection score)

### Conclusion

Hand-crafted poetry from a single author is **highly detectable** when compared to human poetry from Poetry Foundation. The result demonstrates **strong stylometric and semantic separation** but should not be overgeneralized to:
- Other hand-crafted authors
- LLM-generated poetry
- Adversarially-crafted AI poetry
- Different human poetry corpora

The detected patterns are **interpretable and explainable** (punctuation, line structure, vocabulary consistency), making this a promising foundation for more robust authorship attribution research.
